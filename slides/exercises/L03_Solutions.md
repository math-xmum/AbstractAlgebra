## Problem 1. The Rotation Group of the Tetrahedron

**Problem.**

Let $G$ be the rotation group of a regular tetrahedron.

a. Show that each rotation permutes the four faces, so there is a homomorphism
$$\varphi : G \to S_4.$$

b. Prove that $\varphi$ is injective.

c. Show that $|G| = 12$.

d. Conclude that $G \cong A_4$.

e. Find the numbers of Sylow $2$-subgroups and Sylow $3$-subgroups of $A_4$.

f. Explain geometrically what these Sylow subgroups correspond to.

**Solution.**

a. A rotation sends each face to another face, so it induces a permutation of the 4 faces. Composition of rotations matches composition of permutations, hence $\varphi:G\to S_4$ is a homomorphism.

b. If a rotation fixes all four faces, then it fixes all face centers and therefore all vertices. Hence it is the identity, so $\ker\varphi=\{e\}$.

c. There is 1 identity rotation. For each of the 4 choices of vertex-opposite-face axis, there are two nontrivial rotations ($120^\circ$ and $240^\circ$), giving 8. For each of the 3 pairs of opposite edges, there is one $180^\circ$ rotation, giving 3 more. Total: $1+8+3=12$.

d. Thus $\varphi(G)$ is a subgroup of $S_4$ of order 12. The image consists of even permutations, so it must be $A_4$. Hence $G\cong A_4$.

e. Since $|A_4|=12=2^2\cdot 3$, Sylow gives
$$n_3 \mid 4,\quad n_3\equiv 1 \pmod 3,$$
so $n_3=4$.
Also
$$n_2 \mid 3,\quad n_2\equiv 1 \pmod 2,$$
so $n_2=1$ or $3$. In fact $A_4$ has three subgroups of order 4, so $n_2=3$.

f. The Sylow 3-subgroups are generated by $120^\circ$ rotations around a vertex-face axis. The Sylow 2-subgroups correspond to the Klein four subgroup formed by the three $180^\circ$ rotations together with the identity.

## Problem 2. The Rotation Group of the Cube

**Problem.**

Let $G$ be the rotation group of a cube.

a. Show that each rotation permutes the four body diagonals, so there is a homomorphism
$$\psi : G \to S_4.$$

b. Prove that $\psi$ is injective.

c. Show that $|G| = 24$.

d. Conclude that $G \cong S_4$.

e. Use Sylow theory to determine the numbers of Sylow $2$-subgroups and Sylow $3$-subgroups of $G$.

f. Describe geometrically one Sylow $3$-subgroup and one Sylow $2$-subgroup.

**Solution.**

a. A rotation permutes the 4 body diagonals, so we get a homomorphism $\psi:G\to S_4$.

b. If a rotation fixes all four body diagonals, then it fixes the cube rigidly, so it must be the identity. Thus $\psi$ is injective.

c. Count rotations:
- identity: 1
- face-center axes: for each of 3 axes, rotations by $90^\circ$, $180^\circ$, $270^\circ$ give 9
- vertex axes: for each of 4 axes, rotations by $120^\circ$, $240^\circ$ give 8
- edge-center axes: for each of 6 axes, one $180^\circ$ rotation gives 6
Total: $1+9+8+6=24$.

d. Therefore $\psi(G)$ is a subgroup of $S_4$ of order 24, hence $\psi(G)=S_4$. So $G\cong S_4$.

e. Since $|S_4|=24=2^3\cdot 3$, Sylow gives
$$n_3 \mid 8,\quad n_3\equiv 1 \pmod 3,$$
so $n_3=1$ or $4$; in fact $n_3=4$.
Also
$$n_2 \mid 3,\quad n_2\equiv 1 \pmod 2,$$
so $n_2=1$ or $3$; in fact $n_2=3$.

f. A Sylow 3-subgroup is generated by a $120^\circ$ rotation around a body diagonal. A Sylow 2-subgroup can be seen as the subgroup of rotations preserving a chosen pair of opposite faces, isomorphic to the symmetries of a square coming from that axis configuration.
